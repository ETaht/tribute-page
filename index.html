Juri Taht

This remarkable man from Estonia stands as one of the country's most successful cellists, yet unfortunately remains relatively unknown to the people of his motherland today

Here's a time line of his life:

  • 1930 - born in Tartu, Estonia (26.V)
  • 1936 - starts with his cello studies
  • 1941 - emigrates during WWII to Germany
  • 1943 - wins second place in Württemburg music competition (cello)
  • 1947 - graduates from Geislingen Gymansium
  • 1950 - ends his studies in Stuttgard School of Music
  • 1951 - emigrates to America
  • 1952 - receives Bachelor of Arts degree from Dana College in Nebraska
  • 1956 - is honorable discharged from the USAF (the Symphony Orchestra)
  • 1956 - marries Carmen Nieländer
  • 1957 - embarkes on a series of concert tours with the Don Shirley Trio
  • 1958 - receives master´s degree from Manhattan School of Music and started his freelance cellist career
  • 1959 - is accepted to the fraternity Revelia
  • 1976 - becomes a father of a son
  • 1977 - optaines the lead cellist position in Bradway musical "Annie"
  • 1983 - ends his cellist career and moves to Mariland, starts his career in VOA in DC
  • 1992 - is elected the acting Chief of the Estonian Service of VOA
  • 1996 - retires
  • 1997 - initiates the Estonian-Revelia Academic Fund (ERAF) Harald Raudsepp Scolarships give out to students in Estonia
  • 2015 - emigrates back to Estonia
  • 2019 - has his 15 minutes of fame in Estonia (after the movie Green Book premieres)
  • 2022 - dies at the age of 92 (29.XI)

"A star shining in the sky, the existence of which no one had noticed before, lit up before its final extinguishing to burn as bright as a flame. That 15 minutes of fame that not all of us get, although some really deserve it.”

-- Evelin Taht
